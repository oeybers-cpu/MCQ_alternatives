Alternative Gen to MCQ

Generative AI offers several innovative alternatives to traditional multiple-choice questions in academic literacy assessment:

Formative and Dialogic Assessment

GenAI tools enable interactive questioning, explanations, and iterative learning feedback, fostering students' ability to critically assess AI-generated outputs and develop higher-order cognitive skills rather than rote memorization typical of MCQs.

Enhanced Assessment Authenticity and Complexity

Unlike MCQs that often test recall or test-taking skills, GenAI assessments can incorporate open-ended, personalized tasks that demand interpretation, synthesis, and constructive use of information in academic literacy contexts.

Development of Evaluative Judgment

Using GenAI in formative tasks helps students build skills to evaluate and judge information and AI outputs, which MCQs generally cannot provide detailed feedback on.

Customization and Scaffolding

GenAI can tailor tasks, provide scaffolding, and guide students through complex literacy tasks, enabling differentiated learning support that MCQs are unable to offer.

Innovative Assessment Design

Educators leverage GenAI to create assessments that assess multiliteracies and multimodal academic skills more effectively than MCQs, which are limited in assessing such multidimensional competencies.

Limitations of MCQs in Academic Literacy

Memorization Focus

Promote memorization or factual recall over deep understanding

Limited Feedback

Provide limited feedback, typically only right or wrong

Test-Taking Skills

Test test-taking skills rather than true comprehension

Higher-Order Thinking

Ineffective in measuring higher-order thinking or multiliteracies
