Alternatives to MCQ

Academic literacy: identity, disciplinary knowledge & empowering tool

Formative and Dialogic Assessment

GenAI tools enable interactive questioning, explanations, and iterative learning feedback, fostering students' ability to critically assess AI-generated outputs and develop higher-order cognitive skills rather than rote memorisation typical of MCQs.

Enhanced Assessment Authenticity and Complexity

Unlike MCQs that often test recall or test-taking skills, GenAI assessments incorporate open-ended, personalised tasks demanding interpretation, synthesis, and constructive use of information in academic literacy contexts.

Development of Evaluative Judgment

Using GenAI in formative tasks helps students build skills to evaluate and judge information and AI outputs, which MCQs cannot provide detailed feedback on.

Customisation and Scaffolding

GenAI can tailor tasks, provide scaffolding, and guide students through complex literacy tasks, enabling differentiated learning support that MCQs are unable to offer.

Innovative Assessment Design

Educators leverage GenAI to create assessments that evaluate multiliteracies and multimodal academic skills more effectively than MCQs, which are limited in assessing multidimensional competencies.
